Bridgewater-Raritan High School TSA

About Our Chapter

The Bridgewater- Raritan High School’s TSA chapter was established in 2017, by the efforts of a determined group of 6 officers. The officers decided to start a new TSA Chapter at the school because of the diverse competitions TSA offers to the students. From the day it was established, the club drew the eyes of many students, currently having 50 members, a strong base for a newly established club and a number that can be expected to grow in following years.

Activities

Fundraisers


Every few weeks, the club raises funds through a variety of bake sales throughout the high school campus. The students in the chapter even came up with the innovative idea of selling hot chocolate to students during the winter season. The hot chocolate sales were an instant hit, especially since Bridgewater- Raritan High School is an open air campus. The idea, never attempted before at the school, helped raise great funds for the club.

Reaching Out


The BRHS TSA Chapter fosters learning and support between its members and community. The chapter reaches out to parents to help aid students in events they may be preparing for. For example, parents who worked as computer science engineers assisted members in Coding and Software Development.

Future Plans


The Bridgewater-Raritan TSA chapter plans to connect with its community and spread STEM in a variety of ways. The club encourages individuals to partake in STEM activities such as robotics or the Science Bowl, and to volunteer in STEM events such as with FLL. FLL (FIRST LEGO League) is an organization dedicated to introducing children to science, technology, and robotics.  

School System

Education


The motto of the Bridgewater- Raritan High school is “Pursuing Excellence in Education”, and strides taken to uphold this motto can be seen in the school’s status as a nationally recognized blue ribbon school of excellence. Schools receiving this award from the U.S Department of Education display high overall academic excellence, and they exhibit that all students can attain great achievements.
